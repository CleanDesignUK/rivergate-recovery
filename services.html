My Comprehensive Services

I offer a flexible, single-person, online approach to addiction recovery—no inpatient facility or rotating staff. Below you’ll find details about my services, including therapy models, pricing, and how I incorporate unused keywords for a holistic recovery experience.

Keywords & Additional Services

Over time, I’ve noticed certain terms—like caffeine addiction, caffeine dependence, or painkiller addiction—can play a crucial role in someone’s recovery journey. Even individuals identified as a high functioning alcoholic, or those seeking help me stop drinking alcohol, might also grapple with multiple relapse triggers, or find themselves living with a loved one’s addictive behaviour. My services extend to these often overlooked challenges, addressing unique problems like caffeine misuse, co-occurring anxiety or depression, and overlapping dependency issues.

As a qualified online therapist, I can help you navigate less-discussed addictions and concerns, including living with an alcoholic partner, exploring new coping mechanisms, or engaging with drug helpline referrals in your local area. This broad lens ensures no one falls through the cracks due to under-explored or “less common” issues.

Therapy Practices & Techniques

Beyond 12-Step, CBT, and ACT, I integrate other proven methods to tailor each session to your unique needs. Whether dealing with substance misuse, emotional triggers, or complex family dynamics, I draw from:

  • Motivational Interviewing (MI) for building internal motivation
  • Mindfulness & Relaxation Techniques to manage cravings
  • Relapse Prevention Planning addressing specific risk factors
  • Family Systems Approach for improved communication
  • Brief Solution-Focused Techniques for immediate problem-solving
  • Trauma-Informed Care fostering a safe, supportive environment

My single-person, online model ensures consistent care and a **confidential** setting. Each technique is adapted to fit your personal circumstances, building a balanced, sustainable approach to long-term recovery.
